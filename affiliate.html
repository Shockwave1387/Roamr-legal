Affiliate Disclosure

Last updated: March 15, 2026

📋 FTC Disclosure (Required by US Federal Law): Roamr participates in affiliate marketing programs. This means we may earn a small commission when you click certain links in the app and make a purchase or booking. This comes at no additional cost to you — you pay the same price regardless. This disclosure is made in compliance with the Federal Trade Commission's guidelines on endorsements and testimonials (16 CFR Part 255).

How We Make Money

Roamr is completely free for users. We sustain the app through affiliate partnerships with travel companies. When you click a booking link in Roamr (such as "Search on Google Flights" or "Book on Booking.com") and complete a purchase, we may receive a small referral commission from that company.

These commissions are typically:

  • Hotels: 3–8% of the booking value
  • Car rentals: 3–6% of the rental value
  • Flights: $0.50–$3 per ticket (varies by airline)

This revenue helps us keep Roamr free and continue improving it for everyone.

Our Editorial Independence

Affiliate relationships do not influence the travel recommendations, itineraries, or suggestions Roamr generates. Our AI creates travel plans based solely on your preferences and destination — not on which company pays the highest commission.

We show you multiple booking options (Google Flights, Kayak, Expedia, etc.) simultaneously so you can compare and choose the best deal. We never hide or demote results to favor higher-paying partners.
